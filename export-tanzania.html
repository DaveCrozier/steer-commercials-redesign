Route

Built for the Central Corridor and the mining sector.

Most trucks (lorries) we export to Tanzania go on to work the Central Corridor — from Dar es Salaam through Dodoma to Kigoma, with onward trade into Rwanda, Burundi, Zambia and eastern DR Congo. A large share also goes straight into Tanzania's mining regions around Geita and Mererani, where tippers and heavy tractor units see year-round demand.

  • Long-haul tractor units for Central Corridor freight
  • Tippers for mining, quarry and road construction work
  • Rigid lorries for regional distribution fleets
  • All units RHD as standard — no conversion required

FAQ

Tanzania import & shipping questions.

Are UK used trucks right-hand drive, like Tanzanian trucks?

Yes. Tanzania drives on the left, the same as the UK, so UK-sourced trucks (lorries) are right-hand drive as standard — no steering conversion is needed.

Is there an age limit on used trucks imported into Tanzania?

Tanzania applies import restrictions and duty bands based on vehicle age. Rules and thresholds change, so always confirm current Tanzania Revenue Authority (TRA) requirements with your clearing agent before ordering.

Which port do trucks arrive at?

Trucks exported from the UK to Tanzania are shipped to the Port of Dar es Salaam, the gateway to the Central Corridor serving Dodoma, Kigoma, and onward trade routes into Rwanda, Burundi, Zambia and eastern DR Congo.

Do you offer hire purchase or financing?

We don't provide financing directly, but many buyers arrange hire purchase or asset finance through their own bank before ordering — we're happy to provide a pro-forma invoice and vehicle specification to support a finance application.
