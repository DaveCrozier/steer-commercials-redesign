Ghana requires left-hand-drive vehicles

Ghana switched to right-hand traffic in 1974, and right-hand-drive imports are restricted under customs law — any approved exception must be converted to left-hand drive before it can be used on Ghanaian roads. UK trucks are right-hand drive as standard, so we agree LHD sourcing or conversion with buyers before shipping, not after arrival.

FAQ

Ghana import & shipping questions.

Does Ghana allow right-hand drive trucks?

Generally no. Ghana drives on the right, and importing right-hand-drive vehicles is restricted under the Customs, Excise and Preventive (Management) Act — any approved exception must be converted to left-hand drive before use on Ghanaian roads. UK trucks are right-hand drive as standard, so we agree LHD sourcing or conversion with buyers before shipping.

Is there an age limit on used trucks imported into Ghana?

A commonly cited benchmark is 10 years from year of manufacture, with escalating duty surcharges for older vehicles enforced by the Ghana Revenue Authority. This figure is widely reported by shipping agents rather than stated as a single fixed number by GRA itself, so always confirm current rules with GRA or your clearing agent.

Which port do trucks arrive at?

Trucks exported from the UK to Ghana are shipped to the Port of Tema, the country's main gateway and a key transit point for haulage into landlocked neighbours such as Burkina Faso, Mali and Niger.

Do you offer financing or hire purchase?

We don't provide financing directly, but we're happy to provide a pro-forma invoice and full vehicle specification if you're arranging asset finance through your own bank.

Import rules, age limits and LHD requirements change — this page is a starting guide, not customs or legal advice. Confirm current requirements with GRA or your clearing agent before purchase.
