Route

Built for Kampala and the border crossing at Malaba.

Uganda is landlocked, so trucks land at Mombasa and travel the Northern Corridor through Nairobi before crossing into Uganda at Malaba or Busia, continuing on to Kampala. Demand splits between haulage operators running the corridor itself and buyers moving agricultural produce and general freight around Kampala and the wider region.

  • Long-haul tractor units for Mombasa–Kampala freight
  • Tippers and rigids for Kampala construction & distribution
  • Trucks suited to agricultural produce haulage
  • All units RHD as standard — no conversion required

FAQ

Uganda import & shipping questions.

Are UK used trucks right-hand drive, like Ugandan trucks?

Yes. Uganda drives on the left, the same as the UK, so UK-sourced trucks (lorries) are right-hand drive as standard — no steering conversion is needed.

How does the truck reach Uganda if it's landlocked?

Trucks are shipped to the Port of Mombasa in Kenya, then driven or transported inland along the Northern Corridor, crossing the border at Malaba or Busia into Uganda and on to Kampala.

Is there an age limit on used trucks imported into Uganda?

Uganda applies import duty structures that vary with vehicle age. Rules change, so always confirm current Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) requirements with your clearing agent before ordering.

Do you offer hire purchase or financing?

We don't provide financing directly, but many buyers arrange hire purchase or asset finance through their own bank before ordering — we're happy to provide a pro-forma invoice and vehicle specification to support a finance application.
