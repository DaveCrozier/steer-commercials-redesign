European vs Japanese trucks: which should you buy?

7 min readChoosing a truckUpdated 2026

Isuzu, Hino and Mitsubishi Fuso are the trucks most African fleets grew up on — and for good reason. We stock both those brands and UK-sourced European trucks (Volvo, Scania, DAF), so this isn't a pitch for one over the other. Here's an honest look at where each makes sense.

Let's be upfront about Japanese trucks' strengths

Isuzu, Hino and Fuso earned their reputation the hard way: decades of parts availability across East and West Africa, mechanics who know them inside out, and running costs that are hard to beat on shorter, stop-start routes. If your operation depends on being able to find a part in any regional town by lunchtime, that network advantage is real, and we won't pretend otherwise.

Where European trucks change the equation

What UK-sourced European tractor units offer is different, not universally better: more power and torque per tonne, cabs built for drivers who are on the road for days rather than hours, and — because you're buying a well-maintained used truck rather than a new one — a price that competes directly with a new Japanese equivalent.

Power and payload for the corridor

Long-haul routes like the Northern and Central Corridors involve steep gradients, heavy overloading in practice, and multi-day runs. A Volvo FH500 or Scania R520 V8 is built for exactly that kind of continuous heavy haulage — European manufacturers have historically engineered around long-distance, high-tonnage work in a way that shows on routes like these.

Driver comfort, over multiple days

A cab that's comfortable for an 8-hour local run and one that's comfortable for three days on the Mombasa–Kampala road are different problems. European sleeper cabs are built for the second — which matters for driver retention and safety on long-haul work, not just comfort for its own sake.

The affordability angle

This is the part that surprises most buyers: a well-maintained 5–8 year old European tractor unit typically costs a fraction of a brand-new Japanese equivalent, while offering more power and a full UK service history. You're not choosing between "premium" and "affordable" — a used European truck is often both, compared to buying new.

A practical way to decide

  • Running long-haul corridor routes, heavy tippers, or mining haulage? European power and cab comfort tend to pay for themselves.
  • Operating in a remote area where same-day parts matter most? An established Japanese-brand network may be the safer choice — we carry Isuzu, Hino, Fuso and Sinotruk stock directly for exactly this reason.
  • Comparing against a new truck purchase? A used European unit with full service history is worth pricing against the new-Japanese alternative before you decide.
  • Running a mixed fleet already? Many of our buyers take an Isuzu or Hino from our range for daily distribution, paired with a European tractor unit for the heaviest, longest routes.

Parts availability and pricing vary by country and region — the comparisons above are general guidance based on how our buyers typically use each type, not a guarantee for your specific route or location.
